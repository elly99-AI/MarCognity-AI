## Epistemic Integrity Stress Test

### 1 Purpose of the Test

The purpose of this stress test is to evaluate the epistemic robustness of a text generated by a closed autoregressive model (Muse Spark). The text produced is subjected to external validation (MarCognity-AI) in order to make the 'fracture' between linguistic coherence and epistemic foundation observable, identifying the residual regime of uncertainty (Epistemic Boundary) that remains despite the apparent authoritativeness of the tone.
The goal is not to compare models, but:
Clinical content is not evaluated.
The analysis concerns exclusively the epistemic structure of the text.

The procedure is fully reproducible and can be replicated with any closed autoregressive model and any independent external validator.

### Phase 1: Problem Analysis – Fundamental Concepts
Sepsis is not merely a "severe infection," but a life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. It is a heterogeneous clinical syndrome at the interface of microbiology, immunology, hemodynamics, and systems biology.

### Interconnected Key Concepts

| Concept | Operational Definition | Measurement Tools |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Infection** | Tissue invasion by pathogenic microorganisms | Cultures, PCR, imaging |
| **Dysregulated Response** | Imbalance between pro-inflammation ($TNF-\alpha$, $IL-1$, $IL-6$) and anti-inflammation ($IL-10$), featuring endothelial damage, coagulopathy, and late immunosuppression | Biomarkers: PCT, IL-6, LBP |

| **Septic Shock** | A subset of sepsis with persistent hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain MAP $\ge 65$ mmHg and serum lactate $> 2$ mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation | **Sepsis-3** Criteria |


> **Core Pathophysiology:** Glycocalyx-microcirculatory damage leads to loss of endothelial barrier function $\rightarrow$ *capillary leak* $\rightarrow$ interstitial edema $\rightarrow$ tissue hypoxia. This persists even after macrocirculation is normalized. "Continuously giving fluids just because blood pressure is low" is a significant clinical error.

### Phase 2: Theoretical and Mathematical Development – Models and Critical Comparison

### 2.1 Diagnostic Models: Sepsis-3 vs. Phoenix 2024 Pediatric

| Aspect | Sepsis-3 (2016, Adults) | Phoenix (2024, Pediatric) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Philosophy** | Focus on organ dysfunction (abandons SIRS) | Focus on organ dysfunction via PSS |
| **Sepsis Criterion** | Suspected infection + $\uparrow$ SOFA $\ge 2$ | Suspected infection + Phoenix Sepsis Score $\ge 2$ |
| **Shock Criterion** | Sepsis + vasopressors + lactate $> 2$ | Sepsis + Cardiovascular PSS $\ge 1$ |
| **Advantages** | High specificity, reduces overdiagnosis | Increased specificity, PICU standardization |
| **Limitations** | Low early sensitivity | May delay diagnosis of septic shock |

**Critical Comparison:** The 2024 criteria improve specificity but sacrifice early sensitivity. This creates tension between definitional rigor for research and the need for *time-dependent* empirical treatment in clinical practice.

### 2.2 Quantitative Model: Time-Dependence of Mortality
The relationship between antibiotic delay and mortality can be approximated by an exponential function:

$$P_{death}(t) = P_0 \cdot e^{k \cdot t}$$

Where:
- $P_0$ is the baseline mortality.
- $t$ is the delay in hours.
- $k \approx 0.08/h$ according to retrospective analyses.

Every hour of delay increases mortality by approximately **7.6%**.

### 2.3 Therapeutic Pillars and Controversies
* **Antimicrobial Therapy:** Administration within 1 hour for septic shock. Ambiguity: broad-spectrum vs. stewardship. (J-SSCG 2024 dedicated area).
* **Fluid Resuscitation:** Crystalloids 30 mL/kg within 3 hours, but guided by *fluid responsiveness*. Limit: overload $\rightarrow$ increased mortality (**fluid tolerance** concept).
* **Vasopressors:** Norepinephrine as first choice for MAP $\ge 65$ mmHg. Vasopressin as an adjunct in refractory shock.
* **Source Control:** Drainage/surgery. J-SSCG 2024 guidelines highlight this as a primary area.

### Phase 5: Synthesis – Key Points and Practical Applications
* **Definition:** Sepsis = infection + organ dysfunction from dysregulated response. Septic shock = sepsis + refractory hypotension + hyperlactatemia.
* **Diagnosis:** SOFA $\ge 2$ for adults, Phoenix $\ge 2$ for pediatrics. New criteria prioritize specificity over sensitivity.
