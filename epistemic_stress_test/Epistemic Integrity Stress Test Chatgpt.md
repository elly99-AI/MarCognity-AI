### Epistemic Integrity Stress Test

### 1 Purpose of the Test

The purpose of this stress test is to evaluate the epistemic robustness of a text generated by a closed autoregressive (chatgpt) model. The text produced is subjected to external validation (MarCognity-AI) in order to make the 'fracture' between linguistic coherence and epistemic foundation observable, identifying the residual regime of uncertainty (Epistemic Boundary) that remains despite the apparent authoritativeness of the tone.
The goal is not to compare models, but:
Clinical content is not evaluated.
The analysis concerns exclusively the epistemic structure of the text.

The procedure is fully reproducible and can be replicated with any closed autoregressive model and any independent external validator.

 Problem Analysis

### 1.1 Objective Law

Objective law is defined as:

$D = \{\text{legal rules}\}$

Namely:
* A systematic set of rules
* Hierarchically organized
* Endowed with enforceability

👉 *Example:* "Italian law permits divorce."

### 1.2 Subjective Rights

A subjective right is defined as:

$DS = \text{entitlements/powers recognized to a subject}$

It consists of:
* An individual legal position
* Guaranteed by the legal system
* Aimed at satisfying an interest

👉 *Example:* "The creditor has the right to payment."

### 1.3 Legal Relationships

A legal relationship is a relation between subjects regulated by law:

$R = (S_1, S_2, N)$

Where:
* $S_1, S_2$: subjects
* $N$: the rule governing the relationship

### 1.4 Subjective Legal Positions

* **Active (Advantageous)**
  * Subjective right
  * Authority (*potestà*)
* **Passive (Disadvantageous)**
  * Obligation
  * Burden (*onere*)

👉 This distinction introduces a dual structure:

$$\text{Active} \leftrightarrow \text{Passive}$$

### Phase 2: Theoretical Development and Model Comparison

### 2.1 Theories of Subjective Rights

#### Will Theory (Savigny)
A subjective right is the power of the will:

$DS = \text{legally protected will}$

* **✔ Advantages:** Values individual autonomy.
* **✖ Limits:** Fails to explain rights without will (e.g., minors).

#### Interest Theory (Jhering)
A subjective right protects an interest:

$DS = \text{interest} + \text{legal protection}$

* **✔ Advantages:** More realistic; applicable to vulnerable subjects.
* **✖ Limits:** Difficulty in defining "interest".

#### Mixed Theory (Modern)
$DS = \text{power} + \text{interest}$

👉 This is the prevailing theory today.

### 2.2 Structural Analysis of Subjective Rights

A subjective right can be modeled as:

$DS = \{\text{entitlement}_1, \text{entitlement}_2, ..., \text{entitlement}_n\}$

*Example: Right of Property*
* To use
* To enjoy
* To dispose

👉 This confirms the idea in the text: law is a "circumscribed area."

### 2.3 Relationship Between Objective Law and Subjective Rights

$$\text{Subjective Rights} \subset \text{Objective Law}$$

The subjective right:
* Derives from objective law
* Does not exist autonomously

👉 *Theoretical Ambiguity:* Does the law create subjective rights? Or does it merely recognize them (natural law theories)?

### 2.4 Economic and Sociological Analysis

#### Law and Economics
Subjective rights:
* Reduce uncertainty
* Facilitate exchange
* Define property

According to Coase: Efficiency $\rightarrow$ depends on well-defined rights.

#### Sociology of Law
Legal relationships:
* Reflect social structures
* Can diverge from actual practice

👉 *Example:* Formal law $\neq$ lived law.

### 2.5 Authority (*Potestà*) vs. Subjective Rights
* **Subjective Right** $\rightarrow$ Own interest
* **Authority (*Potestà*)** $\rightarrow$ Power exercised for someone else's interest

👉 *Example:* Parent $\rightarrow$ authority over children.

### 4. Method
**Text Generation**
The chatgpt model receives a complex prompt and produces a structured response. No epistemic instructions (requests for verification, sources, stages, etc.) are provided.

**External Validation**
The generated text is sent to MarCognity-AI. An epistemic analysis protocol is applied based on:
- Identification of claims
- Assessment of their verifiability
- Detection of omissions
- Classification of epistemic fractures
- Production of a structured report (CLAIM → STATUS → REASON)

**Analysis of Results**
The analysis focuses on internal consistency, not medical content. No qualitative judgments are made about the models. The goal is to document the observed epistemic behavior.

### 6. Discussion
The test shows that an autoregressive model can generate a very coherent and didactically effective legal text, but without making explicit the degree of verifiability of its statements. External validation (MarCognity-AI) highlights precisely this fracture: it distinguishes between claims and evidence, reports omissions and "epistemic failures" and makes the Epistemic Boundary between linguistic form and epistemic foundation visible.

### 7. Conclusion

The main result is methodological: the generation of the text and its epistemic validation are distinct and complementary processes. A linguistic model can only apparently produce authoritative content; an external supervisor is needed to identify unsupported claims, verifiability limits and epistemic fractures, thus increasing the transparency and robustness of the generated texts.
