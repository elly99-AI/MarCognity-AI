Definition and Characteristics of Legal Norms

Legal norms are rules of conduct imposed on members of society with the aim of preventing or resolving conflicts and ensuring an organized and peaceful social community (Bobbio, 1982). These norms can be of various types, such as written, customary, or religious norms. Legal norms are prescriptive in nature, meaning they impose or prohibit certain behaviors, and are formulated in general and abstract terms (Kelsen, 1967).

Compliance with Norms

Compliance with norms is a fundamental concept in the field of law, as it represents a necessary condition for the functioning of the legal system. Compliance implies that members of society behave in accordance with established rules, avoiding behaviors that could be considered unlawful or harmful to others (Hart, 1961). Compliance can be guaranteed through control and sanction mechanisms, such as punishment for unlawful behavior.

Jurisdiction

Jurisdiction is the power to apply legal norms and resolve conflicts that arise within society (Guarnieri, 2003). Jurisdiction can be exercised by judicial bodies, such as courts, or by other public bodies, such as administrative authorities. The purpose of jurisdiction is to ensure that legal norms are applied uniformly and that conflicts are resolved in a fair and just manner.

Interaction between Compliance and Jurisdiction

The interaction between compliance with norms and jurisdiction is fundamental to the functioning of the legal system. Compliance with norms is a necessary condition for jurisdiction, as there can be no effective jurisdiction without respected norms. Conversely, jurisdiction is the instrument through which legal norms are applied and conflicts are resolved.

Example

An example of how compliance and jurisdiction interact can be seen in the case of an employment contract. The employment contract is a legal norm that governs the working relationship between an employer and an employee. If the employer does not comply with the terms of the employment contract, the employee can take the matter to court to assert their rights. The court, by exercising jurisdiction, applies the norms of the employment contract and resolves the conflict between the parties.

Conclusion

In conclusion, compliance with norms and jurisdiction are two fundamental concepts in the field of law, playing a crucial role in regulating human behavior and resolving conflicts within society. The interaction between these two concepts is essential for the functioning of the legal system.
